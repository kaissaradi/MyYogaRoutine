Kais' Beginner Yoga Routine

Goal

To achieve full flexion and extension, loosen hips and legs, improve balance, and compensate for months of inactivity.

Important Notes for Beginners

  • Focus on proper alignment in all poses.
  • Pay attention to knee lifting in standing poses.
  • Concentrate on hip loosening throughout the routine.
  • Always listen to your body and don't push beyond your limits.

Warm-Up: Seated Poses

1. Easy Seat with Breath Work

Image placeholder

Begin in a comfortable seated position. Focus on your breath, inhaling deeply and exhaling slowly. Read More

Sit cross-legged on the floor with a straight spine, shoulders relaxed. Close your eyes and bring your attention to your breath. Inhale deeply through your nose, allowing your abdomen to expand, and exhale fully, feeling your body relax with each breath. Continue this for several minutes to center your mind and prepare your body for the practice.

2. Knee Circles

Plant your feet on the floor with knees bent. Slowly let your knees fall to the sides on an exhale, and bring them back in on the inhale. Repeat a few times to loosen the hips.

3. Leg Swings

Pick up one leg, clasp your hands under the thigh, and gently swing the leg side to side. This reminds your brain of the knee's range of motion. Switch sides and repeat.

4. Supine Leg Stretch

Lie on your back. Extend one leg and clasp your hands behind the bent leg. Inhale, and on every exhale, switch legs. Repeat 5 times.

Tip: For an extra challenge, keep the extended leg floating above the floor.

5. Waterfall Pose

Image placeholder

Start in bridge pose with a block under your lower back. Float your legs up to the sky. "Floss" the nerve by pointing and flexing your feet.

6. Bridge Pose

Remove the block and stay in bridge pose. Reach towards your heels. Take 2 big, slow breaths, then gently lower your hips.

Transitional Movements

1. Full Body Stretch

Stretch your entire body, then curl into a ball and rock up to a seated position.

2. Tabletop to Downward Dog Transition

Image placeholder

Come into tabletop pose. Drag your right toes back and push back with your hands, leaning into the back heel. Repeat on both sides.

3. Cat-Cow

Inhale to drop the belly (Cow), exhale to round the spine (Cat). Repeat several times.

4. Downward Dog

Curl your toes, hover your knees, and push into Downward Dog. Pedal your heels to loosen hips and hamstrings.

5. Chaturanga Flow

From high plank, lower knees, tuck elbows, and lower to the floor (Chaturanga). Inhale to lift the chest into Cobra, then curl toes and lift into Downward Dog. Repeat 3 times.

Standing Poses

1. Forward Fold and Rag Doll

Walk hands back to toes, knees bent, into a forward fold. Grab your elbows and sway (Rag Doll). Rise with elbows, knees soft, and fold again. Repeat 5 times.

2. Warrior 1

Image placeholder

Step right foot forward, back foot at 45°. Bend the front knee and lift arms. Ensure the bent knee tracks forward. Repeat on the other side.

3. Warrior 2

Image placeholder

Step right foot forward, pivot back foot to 90°. Bend front knee, arms out to a T. Focus on engaging your quads and grounding through your feet.

4. Mountain Pose

Stand with arms at sides, palms out. Ground through feet, engage quads, core, and open chest. Face relaxed. Embody the strength and stability of a mountain.

Hip Opening Poses

1. High or Low Lunge with Twist

Bring hands inside the front leg, swing outside arm out and up. Repeat on both sides.

2. Pigeon Pose

Image placeholder

Enter from Downward Dog or Tabletop. Use props as needed for support.

Cool Down: Supine Poses

1. Knees to Chest

Lie on your back, bring knees to tummy. Rock side to side, then rotate legs in hip circles.

2. Windshield Wipers

Plant feet wide and swing knees side to side like windshield wipers.

3. Supine Twist

Image placeholder

Hug knees to chest, then twist to each side, keeping shoulders grounded.

Final Relaxation: Savasana

Image placeholder

Lie flat on your back, arms and legs relaxed. Close your eyes and focus on your breath. Allow your body to fully relax and integrate the benefits of your practice.

Conclusion

Remember to practice this routine regularly to experience its full benefits. Always listen to your body and modify poses as needed. With consistent practice, you'll notice improvements in flexibility, strength, and overall well-being.
